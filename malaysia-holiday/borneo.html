Borneo Ecotourism

The Untamed Wild Side

Lush Rainforests & Ancient Giants

East Malaysia, consisting of the states of **Sabah** and **Sarawak** on the massive island of Borneo, is an ecotourism paradise. Separated from the Malay Peninsula by the South China Sea, Borneo is home to some of the oldest rainforests in the world (estimated at 140 million years old).

This region is a biodiversity hotspot. Here, you can encounter iconic endangered wildlife like the orangutan, pygmy elephant, and proboscis monkey, and see the world's largest flower, the Rafflesia.

Whether you want to climb the granite peaks of Mount Kinabalu, dive at the world-renowned Sipadan Island, or sleep in a traditional tribal longhouse in Sarawak, Borneo will satisfy your sense of adventure.

Key Highlights:

  • Mount Kinabalu: Malaysia's highest peak at 4,095 meters, popular for high-altitude trekking.
  • Sepilok Rehabilitation Centre: A famous sanctuary in Sandakan dedicated to protecting and rehabilitating orphaned orangutans.
  • Gunung Mulu National Park: A UNESCO site in Sarawak famous for massive limestone caves, karst formations, and million-bat migrations.
  • Kinabatangan River Cruise: Float down the river to spot wild proboscis monkeys, crocodiles, hornbills, and pygmy elephants.

Top National Parks in Borneo

Borneo's national parks are carefully protected reserves. Here are the top ones to visit.

National Park State Primary Attraction Recommended Activities
Kinabalu National Park Sabah Mount Kinabalu, botanical gardens, hot springs, alpine meadows. Summit mountain climb, canopy walks, botanical guided tours.
Gunung Mulu National Park Sarawak Deer Cave, Sarawak Chamber (largest cave room), Pinnacle rock spikes. Show cave walk, adventure caving, canopy walk, bat migration watch.
Bako National Park Sarawak Coastal cliff formations, proboscis monkeys, pitcher plants. Jungle hiking trails, wildlife spotting, overnight camping, boat tours.
Tunku Abdul Rahman Marine Park Sabah Coral reefs, 5 tropical islands close to Kota Kinabalu. Snorkeling, scuba diving, ziplining between islands (Coral Flyer), beach picnics.
