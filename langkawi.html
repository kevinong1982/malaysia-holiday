Langkawi Island

An Archipelago of Paradise

Malaysia's Ultimate Tropical Getaway

Located off the coast of northwestern Malaysia, Langkawi is a collection of 99 islands surrounded by turquoise waters. In 2007, the island was designated a UNESCO Global Geopark due to its stunning ancient rock formations, lush rainforests, and diverse coastal ecosystems.

Langkawi is the perfect holiday for sunseekers and adventurers alike. Whether you want to sunbathe on soft sand, kayak through thick mangrove forests, or enjoy duty-free shopping, Langkawi has it all.

The island's main highlight is the Langkawi Cable Car (SkyCab), which whisks visitors up to the peak of Mount Machinchang, where the breathtaking curved Langkawi SkyBridge hangs high above the canopy.

Highlights and Adventures:

  • Langkawi SkyBridge: A 125-meter curved pedestrian bridge hanging 660 meters above sea level.
  • Kilim Karst Geoforest Park: A mangrove forest filled with eagles, monitor lizards, and limestone caves.
  • Pantai Cenang: The liveliest beach strip on the island with water sports and local bars.
  • Duty-Free Shopping: Stock up on chocolate, perfumes, and kitchenware at tax-free prices in Kuah town.
